## Hacker News — "Show HN" post

**Title:**
> Show HN: Volcan – 111K-param NCA reaches 9.7% on ARC-AGI-2

**Body:**

Hi HN — I've been building a small Neural Cellular Automaton for ARC-AGI-2
and just published the results.

**Headline**: 111,195 parameters, 9.7% exact-match (3/31) on a 30-task
ARC-AGI-2 subset. That's 1.6% of TRM's parameter count at the same 8%
ballpark on this benchmark.

**Architecture**: Five biologically-inspired pillars — anisotropic
directional forces, mycelial small-world attention, Laplacian spectral
tension, a bioelectric ghost-field pre-pattern (inspired by Michael
Levin's morphogenesis work), and apoptotic pruning. Trained via masked
denoising with in-context learning on synthetic tasks generated by an
LLM that *writes Python transform functions* rather than writing demo
grids directly (the latter hallucinates rule-inconsistent demos).

**What actually unlocks the 9.7%**: D8-augmented LoRA-rank-16 test-time
training. Without TTT: 0/31. With TTT + augmentation: 3/31. The MIT TTT
recipe for transformers, ported to an NCA substrate.

**The honest part**: I ran seven controlled ablations — data scale,
MoE capacity, rule-family diversity, LoRA rank, hierarchical macro-cells,
and a HyperNetwork-based meta-learned LoRA initialization — and none
of them moved the number. Two *regressed* it to 2/31 (rank-32 LoRA,
Hyper-TTT). This means the 9.7% is a real ceiling, not tuning noise.

**The Hyper-TTT negative result** might be the most instructive. The
plan was classic meta-learning: train a hypernet to predict good LoRA
init from the 3 demos, so TTT starts 90% of the way to the answer.
Reality: with 90 supervised targets for a 5M-param hypernet, the
meta-init overfits and becomes strictly worse than Kaiming zero-mean.

- Paper (arXiv): [LINK]
- Code + checkpoint: https://github.com/Tompark0927/volcan

Happy to answer questions — especially curious what people think about
whether the ceiling is substrate-bound (local NCA updates can't express
certain rule families) or distribution-bound (synthetic corpus doesn't
overlap enough with real ARC rules).

## r/MachineLearning self-post

**Title:**
> [R] Volcan: a 111K-parameter Neural Cellular Automaton reaches 9.7% on
> ARC-AGI-2, matching TRM (7M) at 1.6% of the parameter count

**Flair:** Research

**Body:**

TL;DR:
- 111,195-parameter Neural Cellular Automaton, five biologically-motivated
  pillars, trained via masked denoising with ICL on LLM-code-dreamed
  synthetic tasks.
- 3/31 = 9.7% exact-match on a held-out ARC-AGI-2 subset.
- Inference recipe: D8-augmented LoRA rank-16 test-time training (MIT TTT
  recipe for transformers, ported to an NCA).
- Seven controlled ablations confirm the 9.7% is a robust ceiling for this
  class of approach. Two ablations (rank-32 LoRA, HyperNetwork meta-init)
  actually regressed to 2/31.

**Relationship to prior work:**
- CompressARC (Liao & Gu 2025, 76K params, 4% on ARC-AGI-2) — no pretraining.
- ARC-NCA (Guichard et al. 2025, ~100K params) — ARC-AGI-1 only.
- TRM (Jolicoeur-Martineau 2025, 7M params, 8% on ARC-AGI-2) — transformer-recursive.
- Volcan sits above both small-model points on a 30-task sample, though CI is
  wide and full-eval accuracy could be anywhere in 5–15%. The efficiency
  point relative to TRM is robust.

**What I think is actually interesting here:**

The eleven systematic experiments (six ceiling-confirming at 3/31, plus the
two regressions, plus three uniformly-zero baseline checks) are more
informative than the headline number. In particular, the HyperNet result
rules out adapter-init quality as the bottleneck — at this data scale a
supervisedly-trained meta-init is strictly worse than zero.

Paper, code, checkpoint, all seven ablation logs:
- Paper (arXiv): [LINK]
- Repo: https://github.com/Tompark0927/volcan
- Architecture doc with 8 weeks of revision log: [docs/architecture.md](...)

Curious what people think about dense scaling vs. distribution-gap as the
next clean experiment.
